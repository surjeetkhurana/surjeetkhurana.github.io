About

My research focuses on artificial intelligence for scientific discovery, with emphasis on materials informatics and physics-informed machine learning. I develop data-driven methods to accelerate the discovery and design of functional materials.

Currently, I work on carbon materials and atmospheric-pressure diamond synthesis, with a focus on machine learning interatomic potentials (MLIPs), atomistic simulations, and self-driving laboratory frameworks.

More broadly, I am interested in AI-driven materials discovery, scalable ML methods for scientific data, and autonomous systems for materials design.
